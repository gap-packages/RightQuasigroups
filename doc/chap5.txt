  [1X5.1 [33X[0;0YTranslations and sections[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YLet [10XQ[110X be a right quasigroup with a parernt of size [10XnP[110X. Right translations of
  right  quasigroups  and  left translations of quasigroups are represented as
  parent permutations in [5XRightQuasigroups[105X, cf. Section [14X4.1[114X. Consequently, such
  translations are elements of the symmetric group on [10X[1..nP][110X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YA  left translation of a right quasigroup [10XQ[110X is not necessarily a permutation
  and  it  is represented as a transformation on [10X[1..nP][110X that fixes all points
  outside  of  [10XParentInd( Q )[110X. Consequently, such translations are elements of
  the full transformation semigroup on [10X[1..nP][110X.[133X

            We  allow  [3Xx[103X to be an element of [3XQ[103X or an element of the underlying
            set   of  [3XQ[103X.  The  right  translation  is  returned  as  a  parent

  [1X5.2 [33X[0;0YMultiplication groups and relative multiplication groups[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  [13Xright multiplication group[113X [23X\textrm{Mlt}_r(Q)[123X of a right quasigroup [23XQ[123X is
  the  permutation  group  generated  by all right translations of [23XQ[123X. The [13Xleft
  multiplication  group[113X [23X\textrm{Mlt}_\ell(Q)[123X of a left quasigroup [23XQ[123X is defined
  dually.  The  [13Xmultiplication  group[113X [23X\textrm{Mlt}(Q)[123X of a quasigroup [10XQ[110X is the
  permutation group generated by all left and right translations of [23XQ[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YIf  [23XS[123X  is  a  subrightquasigroup of a right quasigroup [23XQ[123X, the [13Xrelative right
  multiplication  group[113X  of  [23XS[123X  in  [23XQ[123X  is  the  subgroup  of [23X\textrm{Mlt}_r(Q)[123X
  generated  by  all right translations in [23XQ[123X by elements of [23XS[123X. Note that there
  is a difference betwee the relative right multiplication group of [23XS[123X in [23XQ[123X and
  the  right multiplication group of [23XS[123X. The [13Xrelative left multiplication group[113X
  and the [13Xrelative multiplication group[113X of [23XS[123X in [23XQ[123X are defined analogously.[133X

  [1X5.3 [33X[0;0YInner mappings and inner mapping groups[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YIn  loop  theory,  the  mappings  in  the  multiplication group that fix the
  identity  element are called [13Xinner mappings[113X. Certain standard inner mappings
  of  loops make sense for quasigroups and right quasigroups, when written out
  as products of translations. We will call such mappings of right quasigroups
  inner mappings, too.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWhen  [23XQ[123X  is  a  right  quasigroup  and  [23Xx,y\in  Q[123X,  the  [13Xright inner mapping[113X
  determined by [23Xx[123X and [23Xy[123X is the permutation [23XR(x,y) = R_{xy}^{-1} R_y R_x[123X, where
  we  compose  mappings from right to left. The [13Xright inner mapping group[113X of [23XQ[123X
  is  the  subgroup  of  the right multiplication group [23X\textrm{Mlt}_r(Q)[123X of [23XQ[123X
  generated  by  all  right  inner mappings. When [23XQ[123X is a loop, the right inner
  mapping   group   is   the  stabilizer  of  the  neutral  element  of  [23XQ[123X  in
  [23X\textrm{Mlt}_r(Q)[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YWhen  [23XQ[123X is a quasigroup and [23Xx,y\in Q[123X, the [13Xmiddle inner mapping[113X determined by
  [23Xx[123X  is  the permutation [23XL(x)^{-1} R(x)[123X, and the [13Xleft inner mapping[113X determined
  by [23Xx[123X and [23Xy[123X is the permutation [23XL(x,y) = L_{yx}^{-1} L_y L_x[123X. The [13Xmiddle inner
  mapping  group[113X  of  [23XQ[123X  is  the  subgroup  of  the  multiplication group of [23XQ[123X
  generated by all middle inner mappings. The [13Xleft inner mapping group[113X of [23XQ[123X is
  the  subgroup  of  the  left  multiplication group [23X\textrm{Mlt}_\ell(Q)[123X of [23XQ[123X
  generarted  by  all  left  inner  mappings. When [23XQ[123X is a loop, the left inner
  mapping   grup   is   the   stabilizer  of  the  neutral  element  of  [23XQ[123X  in
  [23X\textrm{Mlt}_\ell(Q)[123X.  Finally,  the [13Xinner mapping group[113X of [23XQ[123X is the subroup
  of  the  multiplication  group  [23X\textrm{Mlt}(Q)[123X of [23XQ[123X generated by all right,
  middle and left inner mappings. When [23XQ[123X is a loop, the inner mapping group is
  the stabilizer of the neutral element of [23XQ[123X in [23X\textrm{Mlt}(Q)[123X.[133X

  [1X5.4 [33X[0;0YDisplacement groups[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor  a  right  quasigroup  [23X(Q,\cdot)[123X, define the [13Xright positive displacement
  group[113X  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_r^+(Q)  =\langle  R_xR_y^{-1}:x,y\in Q\rangle[123X, the [13Xright
  negative  displacement group[113X [23X\mathrm{Dis}_r^-(Q) =\langle R_x^{-1}R_y:x,y\in
  Q\rangle[123X,  and  the  [13Xright  displacement  group[113X  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_r(Q) = \langle
  R_xR_y^{-1},R_x^{-1}R_y:x,y\in Q\rangle[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor a fixed [23Xe\in Q[123X we then have [23X\mathrm{Dis}_r^+(Q)=\langle R_eR_x^{-1}:x\in
  Q\rangle = \langle R_xR_e^{-1}:x\in Q\rangle[123X and [23X\mathrm{Dis}_r^-(Q)=\langle
  R_x^{-1}R_e:x\in Q\rangle = \langle R_e^{-1}R_x:x\in Q\rangle[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  [13Xleft  displacement groups[113X are defined analogously for a left quasigroup
  [23X(Q,\cdot)[123X  by  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_\ell^+(Q)  =\langle L_xL_y^{-1}:x,y\in Q\rangle[123X,
  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_\ell^-(Q)     =\langle    L_x^{-1}L_y:x,y\in    Q\rangle[123X    and
  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_\ell(Q) = \langle L_xL_y^{-1},L_x^{-1}L_y:x,y\in Q\rangle[123X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YFor   a   fixed   [23Xe\in   Q[123X   we   then  have  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_\ell^+(Q)=\langle
  L_eL_x^{-1}:x\in   Q\rangle   =   \langle   L_xL_e^{-1}:x\in   Q\rangle[123X  and
  [23X\mathrm{Dis}_\ell^-(Q)=\langle    L_x^{-1}L_e:x\in    Q\rangle   =   \langle
  L_e^{-1}L_x:x\in Q\rangle[123X.[133X

  [1X5.5 [33X[0;0YRealizing permutation groups as multiplication groups of loops[133X[101X
  
  [33X[0;0YThe  multiplication  group  [23X\mathrm{Mlt}(Q)[123X  of  a loop [23XQ[123X is the permutation
  group  generated  by  all left and right translations of [23XQ[123X, cf. [14X5.2[114X. It is a
  difficult  question in loop theory to determine which permutation groups can
  be  realized  as  multiplication  groups  of  loops. The following functions
  search  for  loops  such  that their multiplication group is contained in or
  equal to a given permutation group [10XG[110X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YSince  the  emphasis  here  is on the multiplication groups, the results are
  returned  not  as  loops  but  as  right sections of loops, cf. [14X5.1[114X. A right
  section [10Xsec[110X contained in the symmetric group on [10X[1..n][110X can then be converted
  to a loop by the command [10XLoopByRightSection( [1..n], sec )[110X.[133X
  
  [33X[0;0YThere are two optional parameters in the searches.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YOne  can speed up the search by setting the argument [10Xdepth[110X higher; the
        price  is  a  much  bigger  memory  consumption. The value of [10Xdepth[110X is
        automatically and optimally chosen if in the permutation group [10XG[110X there
        are  "not  many"  permutations  fixing  [10Xdepth[110X  elements.  The optional
        argument [10Xdepth[110X can be ommmitted, or set to [10Xdepth=2[110X with no harm.[133X
  
  [30X    [33X[0;6YThe  second  optional  argument  [10Xinfolevel[110X  determines  the  amount of
        information   reported   during   the  search.  With  [10Xinfolevel=0[110X,  no
        information  is  reported. With [10Xinfolevel=1[110X, information on timing and
        hits  is  reported. With [10Xinfolevel=2[110X, intermediate results are printed
        as well.[133X
